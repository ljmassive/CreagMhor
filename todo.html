Things to see and do in the area...

Walks

Doon Hill and Fairy Knowe

Take a walk from the apartment to here. Go into main car park, over bridge and follow Manse Road past the cemetery, when the road splits - take the left road. When you go through the forestry gate continue up the hill and the start of the trail is on your left. The small road before this, also on your left is a loop. Especially beautiful in May and June when the blue bells are in full bloom.

By following a series of toadstools along the trail you can learn about the fascinating tale of Reverend Kirk, so enchanted by fairies he spent all of his time studying them, until one day he discovered too much. This is a gorgeous walk, suitable for all ages!

The Lodge, Forest Park Visitor Centre

Situated off the Dukes Pass, less than a mile from the apartment, the Visitor Centre is the best place to start exploring Queen Elizabeth Forest Park, and offers lots for all the family.

This impressive building was gifted to the Forestry Commission in 1960 by the Carnegie Trust. The Lodge offers some of the most spectacular views in the area, including a stunning view across Loch Ard Forest to Ben Lomond.

There are great walks from the centre, including the Highland Boundary Fault trail, a fantastic play area and a wildlife station with CCTV viewing of ospreys, blue tits and many more species (depending on the season).

Loch Ard

This is a stunning area. If you head west along the Main Street of Aberfoyle, and out past the village primary school and church, you will come to a left turn into The Milton. You will find a few parking areas nearby, ideal for a walk around the picturesque Loch Ard and forest area.

If you were to continue, and not turn off for The Milton, you will eventually go through the village of Kinlochard and through again towards Inversnaid. On the way you will see a sign for Stronachlachar. This is the 'other' end of Loch Katrine, where you can walk, cycle and there is a wonderful cafe for brunch, lunch or coffee and cake.

Inversnaid is on the banks of Loch Lomond and has beautiful walks along parts of the West Highland Way and Rob Roys Cave.

Ben A'an

A small mountain (454 m) ideal for all the family, with great views of Loch Katrine, Loch Venacher and Loch Achray.

Directions to FK8 3HY: Head west on Main Street for 0.1 miles, then turn right onto Dukes Pass/A821. Continue along for approximately 5.8 miles, then turn right to stay on Dukes Pass another 0.2 miles, the Car Park will be on your right.

Ben Ledi

Ben Ledi is 879 m high, and is classified as a Corbett. It lies about 6.4 kilometres north-west of Callander, near the village of Kilmahog. It is situated in the Trossachs, an area often regarded as having some of the most romantic scenery in the Highlands.

Directions to FK17 8HF: Head east on Main Street for 0.8 miles, then take the first exit onto the A81 at the Rob Roy roundabout. Stay on the A81 for approximately 10 miles, then turn left onto the A48. After approx. 2.8 miles you will see a turn off for Strathyre Forest Cabins. Turn here, cross over the bridge, the parking area is on the left.

Ben Venue

The name Ben Venue is derived from the Scottish Gaelic words meaning "the miniature mountain". The summit stands at 729 m and lies approximately 2 kilometres south-west of the pier at the southern end of Loch Katrine.

Directions to FK17 8HZ: Head west on Main Street for 0.1 miles, then turn right onto Dukes Pass/A821. Continue along for approximately 5.6 miles, the Car Park will be on your left.

Activities

Go Ape

High-ropes adventure situated off the Dukes Pass at the Forest Park Visitors Centre. Great fun for groups and families with older children (min. age 10 years).

How long does Go Ape Aberfoyle take? On average it takes between 2 and 3 hours to complete a Go Ape Treetop Adventure course. Go Ape Treetop Journey takes 1 hour. This depends on the size of your group and how busy the course is.

Is there a weight limit for Go Ape? The weight limit is listed as 285 pounds, but is dependent on being able to fit in the harness.

Check the Go Ape website for more details, pricing, and bookings.

Briarlands Farm

Family-run farm providing a petting zoo, berry picking, go-karting, a field maze & other activities.

Go to their website for more information, pricing and bookings.

Address: Blair Drummond, Stirling FK9 4UP

Blair Drummond Safari and Adventure Park

Meet Scotland's only giraffes, experience the monkey drive-thru, and immerse yourself in sea lion and bird of prey presentations. Take a boat trip to Chimp Island, explore the amazing dinosaur exhibit and run wild in the giant adventure play area!

Visit their website to plan your day, book, and more.

Address: Blair Drummond, Stirling, FK9 4UR.

Argaty Red Kites

Central Scotland's only Red Kite feeding station, where you can watch the kites from dedicated viewing hides
